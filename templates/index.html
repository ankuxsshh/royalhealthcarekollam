Royal Healthcare, located in Chathannoor, Kollam, Kerala, has been a trusted healthcare provider since 2005. Our mission is simple: to deliver exceptional medical care and improve the health of our community. Over the years, we have earned a reputation for compassionate care, modern facilities, and a dedicated team committed to enhancing the well-being of those we serve. In 2016, we temporarily closed for a comprehensive renovation to upgrade our facilities and expand our services. However, the completion of our plans was delayed, especially due to the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. In response, we offered our facility to the government as a dedicated COVID-19 care center to support local efforts.

Medical Care

Medical care involves services provided by healthcare professionals to maintain or improve health, prevent illness, diagnose conditions, and treat diseases. It includes both physical and mental health support.

Palliative Care

Palliative care is specialized medical care aimed at providing relief from the symptoms and suffering of serious illnesses. It focuses on improving quality of life for patients and their families.

Emergency Care

Emergency care refers to the immediate medical attention provided to individuals experiencing acute health issues or injuries. It aims to stabilize, treat, and prevent further complications in urgent situations.

Fitness and Diet Services

Fitness and diet-based services offer personalized workout plans, nutrition guidance, and wellness coaching to help individuals achieve their health goals and maintain a balanced lifestyle.

Mental & Physical Well-being

Mental and physical well-being counseling supports individuals in managing stress, emotional challenges, and physical health concerns, promoting holistic wellness.

GYNAECOLOGY

A gynecologist specializes in the health of the female reproductive system, diagnosing and treating conditions related to the uterus, ovaries, fallopian tubes, and vagina.

Gynecologists offer services like routine check-ups, cancer screenings, family planning, prenatal care, and treat menstrual disorders and menopausal symptoms, ensuring overall reproductive health.

ORTHOPAEDIC

Orthopedic medicine focuses on diagnosing, treating, and preventing musculoskeletal system disorders, including bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, tendons, and nerves.

Orthopedic surgeons treat fractures, arthritis, sports injuries, and spinal disorders using surgical and non-surgical methods to restore function and alleviate pain.

EMERGENCY MEDICINE

Emergency Medicine specializes in the immediate treatment of acute illnesses or injuries, focusing on rapid evaluation, diagnosis, and stabilization of patients.

Emergency physicians provide care for trauma, cardiac events, respiratory distress, and poisoning, ensuring swift and effective interventions in critical conditions.

CASUALTY

A casualty in a hospital is a patient requiring immediate care due to injury, illness, or emergency, typically triaged in an emergency department.

Casualties are prioritized based on severity, with treatment ranging from minor care to life-saving procedures, ensuring timely and appropriate attention for all patients.

GENERAL SURGERY

General surgery involves a variety of surgical procedures to treat conditions affecting the abdomen, digestive system, thyroid, and soft tissues.

General surgeons perform operations like appendectomies, hernia repairs, and colorectal surgeries to address medical issues and improve patients' health and quality of life.

NEONATOLOGY

Neonatology is a branch of pediatrics specializing in the care of newborns, especially those who are premature, critically ill, or have birth defects.

Neonatologists provide expert care in NICUs, treating conditions like respiratory distress and infections, ensuring healthy development and survival of newborns in their early stages of life.




GENERAL MEDICINE

General medicine in hospitals focuses on diagnosing, treating, and managing a wide range of illnesses and conditions in adult patients.

It serves as the foundation of patient care, addressing common diseases, chronic conditions, and acute illnesses. General physicians collaborate with specialists to ensure comprehensive care, emphasizing diagnosis, prevention, and holistic treatment.

PEDIATRICIAN

A pediatrician in a hospital specializes in the medical care of infants, children, and adolescents.

They diagnose and treat illnesses, provide preventive health services, and monitor growth and development. Working closely with families and other healthcare professionals, pediatricians ensure the physical, emotional, and social well-being of young patients in a nurturing environment.

CARDIOLOGY

Cardiology is a vital medical specialty in hospitals, focusing on diagnosing and treating heart and vascular disorders.

Cardiologists use advanced technology to manage conditions like heart attacks, arrhythmias, and hypertension. Hospitals often feature dedicated cardiac units, offering services such as ECG, angiography, and cardiac surgery, ensuring comprehensive heart care for patients.

PSYCHIATRIST

A psychiatrist in a hospital specializes in diagnosing and treating mental health disorders.

They provide therapy, prescribe medications, and collaborate with multidisciplinary teams to support patients' emotional and psychological well-being. Psychiatrists address conditions like depression, anxiety, schizophrenia, and substance abuse, playing a critical role in comprehensive healthcare and patient recovery.

PULMONOLOGIST

A pulmonologist is a specialized physician in a hospital who diagnoses and treats respiratory system disorders, including asthma, COPD, pneumonia, and lung infections.

They perform diagnostic tests like spirometry and bronchoscopy, manage critical cases in intensive care, and collaborate with other specialists to ensure optimal respiratory health for patients.

E.N.T

The ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) department in a hospital specializes in diagnosing and treating conditions related to the ear, nose, throat, head, and neck.

It addresses issues like hearing loss, sinusitis, tonsillitis, and voice disorders, using advanced medical procedures and surgeries to improve patients' quality of life.

PHYSIOTHERAPY

Physiotherapy focuses on rehabilitation and improving movement, strength, and mobility for patients recovering from injuries, surgeries, or illnesses to enhance their quality of life.

Skilled physiotherapists use techniques like manual therapy and exercises to alleviate pain, promote healing, and restore mobility for conditions like musculoskeletal injuries and neurological disorders.

NEURO MEDICINE

Neuro Medicine involves diagnosing and treating disorders of the brain, spinal cord, and nerves, aiming to improve quality of life for patients with neurological conditions.

Neurologists use advanced tools like MRIs to diagnose conditions such as strokes, epilepsy, and neurodegenerative disorders, offering personalized treatment plans combining medication and therapy.

ANASTHESIA

Anesthesia is used to induce temporary loss of sensation during surgeries, ensuring patient comfort and safety by managing pain and vital signs during procedures.

Anesthesiologists carefully administer anesthesia based on procedure needs, monitoring the patient's condition and providing post-procedure care to ensure recovery and stability.

DIABETOLOGY

Diabetology focuses on diagnosing and managing diabetes and its complications, helping patients manage blood sugar levels to improve health and prevent long-term issues.

Endocrinologists provide personalized care plans, including insulin therapy and continuous glucose monitoring, to manage diabetes and prevent complications such as heart disease or kidney failure.
