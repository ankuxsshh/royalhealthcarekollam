ABOUT US

Royal Healthcare, located in Chathannoor, Kollam, Kerala, has been a trusted healthcare provider since 2005. Our mission is simple: to deliver exceptional medical care and improve the health of our community. Over the years, we have earned a reputation for compassionate care, modern facilities, and a dedicated team committed to enhancing the well-being of those we serve. In 2016, we temporarily closed for a comprehensive renovation to upgrade our facilities and expand our services. However, the completion of our plans was delayed, especially due to the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. In response, we offered our facility to the government as a dedicated COVID-19 care center to support local efforts.

24hrs CASUATY AND TRAUMA CARE

24-hour Casualty and Trauma Care provides round-the-clock emergency medical services for patients suffering from accidents, critical injuries, and life-threatening conditions. Equipped with state-of-the-art medical technology, a dedicated team of doctors, paramedics, and nurses ensures immediate and effective treatment.

The facility offers rapid response for road accidents, heart attacks, strokes, burns, and other medical emergencies. Advanced trauma units, intensive care support, and surgical intervention are available to stabilize and save lives. With a commitment to prompt and expert care, the center operates 24/7 to handle emergencies efficiently, ensuring the best possible outcomes for patients in critical conditions.

GENERAL MEDICINE

General Medicine is a medical specialty that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of a wide range of diseases affecting adults. It covers various conditions related to the heart, lungs, digestive system, nervous system, and other vital organs.

General physicians are trained to manage chronic illnesses like diabetes and hypertension, as well as acute infections and common health issues. They provide comprehensive healthcare, emphasizing patient education, lifestyle modifications, and preventive care. General Medicine serves as the foundation of medical practice, ensuring early detection and appropriate treatment of diseases while coordinating specialized care when needed.

GENERAL SURGERY

General surgery is a medical specialty that focuses on surgical procedures involving the abdomen, digestive tract, skin, soft tissues, and other vital organs. General surgeons are trained to perform operations such as appendectomies, hernia repairs, gallbladder removals, and trauma surgeries.

They also handle conditions affecting the endocrine system, breasts, and vascular structures. In addition to performing surgeries, general surgeons play a crucial role in diagnosing conditions, providing preoperative and postoperative care, and managing surgical emergencies. With advancements in minimally invasive techniques, including laparoscopic and robotic-assisted surgeries, general surgery continues to evolve, offering patients safer and more effective treatment options.

ORTHOPAEDICS

Orthopaedics is a branch of medicine that focuses on diagnosing, treating, preventing, and rehabilitating disorders related to the musculoskeletal system, including bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, and tendons. Orthopaedic specialists, known as orthopaedists, treat conditions such as fractures, arthritis, spinal disorders, sports injuries, and congenital deformities.

Treatments range from medications and physical therapy to surgical procedures like joint replacements and arthroscopy. Advanced technologies, including robotic-assisted surgery and regenerative medicine, have enhanced patient outcomes. Orthopaedics plays a vital role in improving mobility, reducing pain, and enhancing the quality of life for individuals with musculoskeletal issues.

GYNAECOLOGY

Gynaecology is a branch of medicine that focuses on the health of the female reproductive system, including the uterus, ovaries, fallopian tubes, and vagina. It deals with a wide range of conditions such as menstrual disorders, pregnancy, fertility issues, hormonal imbalances, and menopause.

Gynaecologists diagnose and treat infections, cancers, and other reproductive health concerns. Regular check-ups, including Pap smears and ultrasounds, help in early detection of potential issues. Gynaecology also overlaps with obstetrics, which focuses on pregnancy and childbirth. With advancements in medical technology, gynaecological treatments and surgeries have become more effective, improving women's overall health and well-being.

ENT

ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) is a medical specialty focused on diagnosing and treating disorders related to the ear, nose, throat, head, and neck. Also known as Otolaryngology, it covers a wide range of conditions, including hearing loss, sinus infections, tonsillitis, voice disorders, and allergies.

ENT specialists, or otolaryngologists, perform surgeries like tonsillectomies, sinus surgeries, and cochlear implants. They also manage conditions such as vertigo, nasal polyps, and throat cancer. This field integrates both medical and surgical treatments, helping patients with issues affecting breathing, hearing, speech, and balance. ENT specialists play a crucial role in improving overall quality of life.




PEDIATRIC

Pediatrics is a branch of medicine that focuses on the health and well-being of infants, children, and adolescents. Pediatricians diagnose and treat a wide range of illnesses, from minor health issues to serious diseases, ensuring proper growth and development.

They provide preventive care, vaccinations, and guidance on nutrition, behavior, and overall child health. Pediatricians also address congenital conditions, infections, and developmental disorders. Their approach is child-centered, considering both medical and emotional needs. By working closely with parents and caregivers, pediatricians play a crucial role in promoting a healthy childhood and laying the foundation for a strong, disease-free future.

RADIOLOGY

Radiology is a medical specialty that uses imaging techniques to diagnose and treat diseases within the body. It involves the use of technologies such as X-rays, CT scans, MRI scans, ultrasound, and nuclear medicine to create visual representations of internal organs, tissues, and structures.

Radiologists analyze these images to detect conditions like fractures, tumors, infections, and cardiovascular diseases. The field also includes interventional radiology, which involves minimally invasive procedures using imaging guidance for treatments like biopsies, stent placements, and vascular repairs. Radiology plays a crucial role in modern healthcare by enabling early diagnosis, guiding treatment decisions, and improving patient outcomes.

ANASTHESIA

Anesthesia is a medical practice that involves administering drugs to prevent pain during surgeries, procedures, or diagnostic tests. It can be classified into three main types: general anesthesia, which induces a state of unconsciousness; regional anesthesia, which numbs a specific part of the body; and local anesthesia, which numbs a small area.

Anesthetics work by blocking nerve signals, allowing patients to undergo procedures without experiencing pain. Anesthesia is carefully managed by anesthesiologists to ensure patient safety and comfort, considering factors such as the type of procedure, the patient's health, and potential side effects.

CARDIOLOGY

Cardiology is a branch of medicine focused on diagnosing, treating, and preventing heart-related conditions and diseases. It involves a wide range of conditions, from congenital heart defects to acquired diseases such as coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and valvular disorders.

Cardiologists use various diagnostic tools like echocardiograms, ECGs, and stress tests to assess heart health. Treatment options can include lifestyle changes, medications, minimally invasive procedures, and surgeries such as coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG). Preventive cardiology aims to reduce risk factors like high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and smoking to maintain heart health and prevent cardiovascular events.

NEURO MEDICINE

Neuro medicine, also known as neurology, is a branch of medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and management of disorders related to the nervous system. This includes conditions affecting the brain, spinal cord, nerves, and muscles. Neuro medicine encompasses a wide range of neurological diseases such as stroke, epilepsy, Parkinson's disease, Alzheimer's, multiple sclerosis, and peripheral neuropathy.

Neurologists, the specialists in this field, use advanced techniques like imaging, electrophysiological tests, and genetic screenings to understand and treat neurological conditions. Effective neuro medicine can significantly improve patients' quality of life, manage symptoms, and, in some cases, prevent further progression of the disease.

NEURO SURGERY

Neurosurgery is a specialized branch of surgery that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and surgical intervention of disorders affecting the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nervous system. It encompasses a wide range of conditions, including brain tumors, traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord injuries, epilepsy, and neurovascular disorders such as stroke.

Neurosurgeons employ advanced techniques such as minimally invasive surgery, stereotactic surgery, and neuroendoscopy to treat these complex conditions. The field requires a deep understanding of the nervous system's intricate anatomy and function, as well as expertise in critical decision-making to optimize patient outcomes and recovery.

OMF

OMF (Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery) in hospitals focuses on diagnosing and treating conditions affecting the mouth, jaws, face, and neck. It covers a wide range of medical issues, including trauma, tumors, infections, congenital deformities, and cosmetic enhancements.

OMF specialists perform surgeries such as jaw realignment, tooth extractions, and reconstructive procedures for facial injuries. The goal is to restore function, improve aesthetics, and alleviate pain. In hospitals, OMF professionals collaborate with other medical teams, providing comprehensive care, often in emergency or specialized settings. This multidisciplinary approach ensures effective management of complex conditions affecting both health and appearance.

Medical Care

Medical care involves services provided by healthcare professionals to maintain or improve health, prevent illness, diagnose conditions, and treat diseases. It includes both physical and mental health support.

Palliative Care

Palliative care is specialized medical care aimed at providing relief from the symptoms and suffering of serious illnesses. It focuses on improving quality of life for patients and their families.

Emergency Care

Emergency care refers to the immediate medical attention provided to individuals experiencing acute health issues or injuries. It aims to stabilize, treat, and prevent further complications in urgent situations.

Fitness and Diet Services

Fitness and diet-based services offer personalized workout plans, nutrition guidance, and wellness coaching to help individuals achieve their health goals and maintain a balanced lifestyle.

Mental & Physical Well-being

Mental and physical well-being counseling supports individuals in managing stress, emotional challenges, and physical health concerns, promoting holistic wellness.
